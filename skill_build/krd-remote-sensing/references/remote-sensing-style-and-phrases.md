# Remote Sensing Style and Phrases

## When to use this reference

Use this reference when polishing manuscript language, writing section transitions, or converting Chinese technical logic into restrained Remote Sensing-style English.

## Tone Rules

- Be precise and restrained.
- Prefer method-result-interpretation sequencing.
- Avoid inflated novelty claims.
- Use "may", "can", or "suggests" when evidence is inferential.
- State uncertainty and limitations directly.

## Reusable Phrase Patterns

Use as templates, not as copied source text:

- "To address this gap, this study..."
- "The overall workflow is shown in Figure [X]."
- "Accuracy was evaluated using [METRICS]."
- "Compared with [BASELINE], [MODEL] achieved [INSERT VERIFIED RESULT]."
- "This difference may be attributed to [MECHANISM]."
- "The inclusion of DEM-derived variables was evaluated through ablation experiments."
- "Future work should further validate [LIMITATION] across [REGION/DATA]."

## Section Transitions

Introduction to objective:

- "However, the joint role of continuous bare-rock fraction, semantic segmentation, and terrain constraints remains insufficiently tested for cross-region KRD mapping."

Methods:

- "The experiment was designed to keep the feature space consistent across study areas."

Discussion:

- "These results indicate that the contribution of terrain information should be interpreted in relation to both local relief and transfer consistency."

## Prohibited Style

- Do not say "significantly improved" unless statistical or validated improvement is supplied.
- Do not claim "first" without exhaustive literature verification.
- Do not use exact performance values unless supplied by the user or verified in project outputs.
